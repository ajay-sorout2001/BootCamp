Introduction

Hockey is a team sport played on ice or a field, where players use sticks to hit a puck or ball into the opponent's goal. It is known for its speed, skill, and physical intensity.

History

Hockey's origins can be traced back to ancient civilizations. The modern version of ice hockey developed in Canada in the late 19th century. The first organized indoor hockey game was played in 1875 in Montreal.

The National Hockey League (NHL) was founded in 1917 and is now the premier professional ice hockey league in the world.

Types of Hockey

  • Ice Hockey: Played on an ice rink using skates and a puck.
  • Field Hockey: Played on grass or turf with a ball.
  • Roller Hockey: Played on roller skates, often indoors.
  • Floor Hockey: A version of hockey played indoors with a plastic ball and stick.
